Executive Summary¶

This case study analyzes customer purchasing behavior for AeroFit treadmills. The analysis shows that income, fitness level, and usage intensity are the primary drivers of product selection, while age has minimal influence.

KP781 serves a distinct premium segment consisting of high-income, high-fitness, and high-usage customers, whereas KP281 and KP481 cater to mass-market and mid-tier users with similar behavioral patterns.

Final Business Recommendation¶

Product choice at AeroFit is primarily driven by income, fitness level, and usage intensity rather than age.

  • KP281 should be marketed as an entry-level treadmill for beginners.
  • KP481 should be positioned as a comfort-oriented upgrade.
  • KP781 should maintain premium pricing and target performance-focused users.
